            <div class="inventory-analysis">
                <h3>📦 Inventory Valuation Methods</h3>
                <div class="inventory-methods">
                    <div class="method">
                        <h4>1. Cost Method</h4>
                        <p>Values inventory at wholesale cost. Most conservative approach but doesn't reflect retail potential.</p>
                    </div>
                    <div class="method">
                        <h4>2. Market Method</h4>
                        <p>Values inventory at current wholesale replacement cost. Accounts for price fluctuations and market conditions.</p>
                    </div>
                    <div class="method">
                        <h4>3. Net Realizable Value</h4>
                        <p>Values inventory at expected selling price minus selling costs. Most realistic for slow-moving inventory.</p>
                    </div>
                </div>
                
                <h3>📊 Inventory Quality Factors</h3>
                <ul>
                    <li><strong>Turnover Rate:</strong> Higher turnover indicates better inventory management</li>
                    <li><strong>Age Analysis:</strong> Newer inventory commands higher values</li>
                    <li><strong>Seasonal Considerations:</strong> Timing of sale affects inventory value</li>
                    <li><strong>Brand Mix:</strong> Popular brands maintain value better</li>
                    <li><strong>Condition Assessment:</strong> Physical condition impacts realizable value</li>

        <section id="common-mistakes" class="common-mistakes">
            <h2>Common Retail Store Valuation Mistakes to Avoid</h2>
            <p>These mistakes can cost you thousands in undervalued sales:</p>
            
            <div class="mistakes-list">
                <div class="mistake">
                    <h3>❌ Using Revenue Instead of SDE</h3>
                    <p><strong>Mistake:</strong> Valuing based on gross revenue without considering profitability and owner benefits.</p>
                    <p><strong>Solution:</strong> Always use SDE (Seller's Discretionary Earnings) which accounts for owner benefits and provides a true picture of cash flow available to a new owner.</p>
                </div>
                
                <div class="mistake">
                    <h3>❌ Overvaluing Inventory</h3>
                    <p><strong>Mistake:</strong> Valuing inventory at retail prices instead of wholesale cost or realistic liquidation value.</p>
                    <p><strong>Solution:</strong> Use conservative inventory valuation methods and account for dead stock, seasonal items, and market conditions.</p>
                </div>
                
                <div class="mistake">
                    <h3>❌ Ignoring Lease Terms</h3>
                    <p><strong>Mistake:</strong> Not considering lease transferability, remaining term, or renewal options in valuation.</p>
                    <p><strong>Solution:</strong> Factor in lease security, transferability, and location value when calculating business worth.</p>
                </div>
                
                <div class="mistake">
                    <h3>❌ Not Accounting for Location Risk</h3>
                    <p><strong>Mistake:</strong> Treating all retail locations equally without considering traffic, competition, and demographics.</p>
                    <p><strong>Solution:</strong> Apply location-based adjustments to multiples based on foot traffic, visibility, and market strength.</p>
                </div>
                
                <div class="mistake">
                    <h3>❌ Ignoring Seasonal Variations</h3>
                    <p><strong>Mistake:</strong> Using peak season data without adjusting for seasonal fluctuations in retail performance.</p>
                    <p><strong>Solution:</strong> Use 12-month trailing data and normalize for seasonal patterns to get accurate valuations.</p>
                </div>
            </div>
        </section>
