        <section id="common-mistakes" class="common-mistakes">
            <h2>Common SaaS Valuation Mistakes to Avoid</h2>
            <p>These mistakes can cost you millions in undervalued sales:</p>
            
            <div class="mistakes-list">
                <div class="mistake">
                    <h3>‚ùå Using Revenue Instead of ARR</h3>
                    <p><strong>Mistake:</strong> Valuing based on total revenue including one-time fees and non-recurring income.</p>
                    <p><strong>Solution:</strong> Always use ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue) which represents predictable, recurring revenue that buyers value most.</p>
                </div>
                
                <div class="mistake">
                    <h3>‚ùå Ignoring Churn Rate Impact</h3>
                    <p><strong>Mistake:</strong> Not properly accounting for customer churn in valuation calculations.</p>
                    <p><strong>Solution:</strong> High churn rates significantly reduce valuation multiples. Focus on reducing churn to increase company value.</p>
                </div>
                
                <div class="mistake">
                    <h3>‚ùå Overlooking Customer Concentration Risk</h3>
                    <p><strong>Mistake:</strong> Having too much revenue dependent on a few large customers.</p>
                    <p><strong>Solution:</strong> Diversify customer base and implement customer concentration risk adjustments in valuation.</p>
                </div>
                
                <div class="mistake">
                    <h3>‚ùå Not Accounting for Growth Sustainability</h3>
                    <p><strong>Mistake:</strong> Using peak growth rates without considering long-term sustainability.</p>
                    <p><strong>Solution:</strong> Use sustainable growth rates and consider market saturation in long-term projections.</p>
                </div>
                
                <div class="mistake">
                    <h3>‚ùå Ignoring Market Conditions</h3>
                    <p><strong>Mistake:</strong> Using outdated multiples without considering current market conditions.</p>
                    <p><strong>Solution:</strong> Regularly update valuation multiples based on recent transactions and market sentiment.</p>
                </div>
            </div>
        </section>

            <div class="faq-item">
                <h3>Q: How accurate is your SaaS valuation calculator?</h3>
                <p><strong>A:</strong> Our calculator uses the same ARR methodology trusted by VCs and private equity firms worldwide. While no online calculator can replace a professional appraisal, our results are typically within 10-15% of professional valuations, making it an excellent starting point for planning and negotiations.</p>

            <div class="faq-item">
                <h3>Q: What's the difference between ARR and MRR for SaaS valuation?</h3>
                <p><strong>A:</strong> ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue) is the primary metric for SaaS valuation as it represents predictable, recurring revenue. MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue) is useful for tracking growth trends and calculating ARR (MRR √ó 12). ARR multiples typically range from 5x-10x, while MRR multiples range from 60x-120x.</p>
            </div>
            
            <div class="faq-item">
                <h3>Q: How does churn rate affect SaaS valuation?</h3>
                <p><strong>A:</strong> Churn rate significantly impacts SaaS valuation. High churn rates (above 5% annually for B2B) reduce valuation multiples because they indicate customer dissatisfaction and revenue instability. Low churn rates (under 3% annually) can increase multiples by 1-2x due to predictable revenue growth.</p>
            </div>
            
            <div class="faq-item">
                <h3>Q: What's a good LTV:CAC ratio for SaaS companies?</h3>
                <p><strong>A:</strong> A healthy LTV:CAC ratio is 3:1 or higher, with 5:1+ being excellent. This ratio indicates efficient customer acquisition and strong unit economics. Ratios below 3:1 suggest unsustainable customer acquisition costs that will hurt long-term profitability and valuation.</p>
            </div>
            
            <div class="faq-item">
                <h3>Q: How often should I get my SaaS business valued?</h3>
                <p><strong>A:</strong> We recommend getting a valuation quarterly for fast-growing SaaS companies or whenever you're considering major decisions like selling, seeking investment, or bringing in partners. SaaS markets evolve rapidly, and your business value can change significantly over time.</p>
            </div>
            
            <div class="faq-item">
                <h3>Q: Can I use this calculator for pre-revenue SaaS companies?</h3>
                <p><strong>A:</strong> Our calculator works best for SaaS companies with at least 12 months of revenue data. For pre-revenue companies, valuation is typically based on comparable transactions, intellectual property value, team strength, and market opportunity rather than financial metrics.</p>
            </div>
            
            <div class="faq-item">
                <h3>Q: What's the Rule of 40 and how does it affect valuation?</h3>
                <p><strong>A:</strong> The Rule of 40 states that a healthy SaaS company's growth rate plus profit margin should equal 40% or higher. Companies exceeding the Rule of 40 often command premium valuation multiples, while those below may see discounted multiples due to unsustainable growth or poor unit economics.</p>
            </div>
            
            <div class="faq-item">
                <h3>Q: How do market conditions affect SaaS valuations?</h3>
                <p><strong>A:</strong> Market conditions significantly impact SaaS valuations. During bull markets, multiples can reach 15x+ ARR, while bear markets may see multiples drop to 3x-5x ARR. Interest rates, investor sentiment, and economic conditions all influence SaaS valuation multiples.</p>
