        <section id="common-mistakes" class="common-mistakes">
            <h2>Common Manufacturing Valuation Mistakes to Avoid</h2>
            <p>These mistakes can cost you thousands in undervalued sales:</p>
            
            <div class="mistakes-list">
                <div class="mistake">
                    <h3>❌ Using Revenue Instead of SDE</h3>
                    <p><strong>Mistake:</strong> Valuing based on gross revenue without considering profitability and owner benefits.</p>
                    <p><strong>Solution:</strong> Always use SDE (Seller's Discretionary Earnings) which accounts for owner benefits and provides a true picture of cash flow.</p>
                </div>
                
                <div class="mistake">
                    <h3>❌ Ignoring Equipment Depreciation</h3>
                    <p><strong>Mistake:</strong> Valuing equipment at original cost without considering depreciation and obsolescence.</p>
                    <p><strong>Solution:</strong> Use current market values and consider equipment age, condition, and technology level in valuation.</p>
                </div>
                
                <div class="mistake">
                    <h3>❌ Overlooking Environmental Liabilities</h3>
                    <p><strong>Mistake:</strong> Not accounting for potential environmental cleanup costs and compliance requirements.</p>
                    <p><strong>Solution:</strong> Conduct environmental assessments and factor in potential liabilities in valuation calculations.</p>
                </div>
                
                <div class="mistake">
                    <h3>❌ Not Accounting for Working Capital Needs</h3>
                    <p><strong>Mistake:</strong> Ignoring the working capital required to operate the business post-sale.</p>
                    <p><strong>Solution:</strong> Adjust valuation for working capital requirements, typically 10-20% of annual revenue.</p>
                </div>
                
                <div class="mistake">

                <p><strong>A:</strong> We use current market values for equipment based on age, condition, and technology level. Equipment typically represents 15-35% of total manufacturing business value, depending on the industry and equipment type.</p>
            </div>
            
            <div class="faq-item">
                <h3>Q: What's the difference between SDE and EBITDA for manufacturing valuation?</h3>
                <p><strong>A:</strong> SDE (Seller's Discretionary Earnings) is preferred for manufacturing businesses under $5M in revenue because it adds back owner benefits, making it more accurate for small business valuations. EBITDA is typically used for larger corporations and doesn't account for owner compensation.</p>
            </div>
            
            <div class="faq-item">
                <h3>Q: How does equipment age affect manufacturing business value?</h3>
                <p><strong>A:</strong> Equipment age significantly impacts value. Newer, well-maintained equipment can increase business value by 20-40%, while outdated equipment may require significant capital investment and reduce valuation multiples.</p>
            </div>
            
            <div class="faq-item">
                <h3>Q: How often should I get my manufacturing business valued?</h3>
                <p><strong>A:</strong> We recommend getting a valuation annually or whenever you're considering major decisions like selling, seeking investment, or bringing in partners. Manufacturing markets evolve rapidly, and your business value can change significantly over time.</p>

                <h3>Q: Can I use this calculator for contract manufacturing businesses?</h3>
                <p><strong>A:</strong> Yes, our calculator works for all types of manufacturing businesses including contract manufacturing. However, contract manufacturers may have different risk profiles and multiples due to customer concentration and contract dependency.</p>
            </div>
            
            <div class="faq-item">
                <h3>Q: How do environmental regulations affect manufacturing valuation?</h3>
                <p><strong>A:</strong> Environmental compliance costs and potential liabilities can significantly impact manufacturing valuations. Businesses with environmental issues may see 10-30% reductions in value, while those with strong environmental records may command premium multiples.</p>
            </div>

                <p><strong>A:</strong> Gross margins vary by industry, but generally 25-40% is considered healthy for manufacturing businesses. Higher margins typically command higher valuation multiples, while margins below 20% may indicate operational inefficiencies or pricing pressure.</p>
