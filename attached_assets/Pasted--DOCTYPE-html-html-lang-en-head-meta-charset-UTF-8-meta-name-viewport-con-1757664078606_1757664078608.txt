        <section id="common-mistakes" class="common-mistakes">
            <h2>Common Service Business Valuation Mistakes to Avoid</h2>
            <p>These mistakes can cost you thousands in undervalued sales:</p>
            
            <div class="mistakes-list">
                <div class="mistake">
                    <h3>‚ùå Using Revenue Instead of SDE</h3>
                    <p><strong>Mistake:</strong> Valuing based on gross revenue without considering profitability and owner benefits.</p>
                    <p><strong>Solution:</strong> Always use SDE (Seller's Discretionary Earnings) which accounts for owner benefits and provides a true picture of cash flow available to a new owner.</p>
                </div>
                
                <div class="mistake">
                    <h3>‚ùå Ignoring Client Concentration Risk</h3>
                    <p><strong>Mistake:</strong> Not accounting for the risk of losing major clients or revenue concentration.</p>
                    <p><strong>Solution:</strong> Apply risk discounts for high client concentration and work to diversify your client base to increase business value.</p>
                </div>
                
                <div class="mistake">
                    <h3>‚ùå Overlooking Key Person Dependency</h3>
                    <p><strong>Mistake:</strong> Not considering how dependent the business is on the owner or key employees.</p>
                    <p><strong>Solution:</strong> Document processes, cross-train employees, and reduce key person dependency to increase transferability and value.</p>
                </div>
                
                <div class="mistake">
                    <h3>‚ùå Not Valuing Client Relationships</h3>
                    <p><strong>Mistake:</strong> Treating all revenue equally without considering contract stability and client relationships.</p>
                    <p><strong>Solution:</strong> Document client contracts, retention rates, and relationship strength to demonstrate revenue quality and stability.</p>
                </div>
                
                <div class="mistake">
                    <h3>‚ùå Ignoring Scalability Factors</h3>
                    <p><strong>Mistake:</strong> Not considering the business's ability to grow without proportional cost increases.</p>
                    <p><strong>Solution:</strong> Highlight systems, processes, and technology that enable efficient scaling to justify higher multiples.</p>
                </div>
            </div>
